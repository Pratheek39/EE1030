Given 
\begin{align}
	\norm{\vec{a}}= \norm{\vec{b}}, {\cos\theta} = \frac{1}{2}, 
	\vec{a}^{\top}{\vec{b}} = \frac{1}{2},  \\
\implies 
	\frac{1}{2} = \frac{\frac{1}{2}}{\norm{\vec{a}}^2}
\implies \norm{\vec{a}}
= \norm{\vec{b}}=1
\end{align}
by using  the definition of the scalar product
	in \eqref{eq:angle-inner}.
