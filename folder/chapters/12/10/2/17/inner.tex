    \begin{align}
         \vec{B} - \vec{A} = \myvec{-1\\3\\5},\, 
         \vec{C} - \vec{B} = \myvec{-1\\-2\\-6},\,
         \vec{C} - \vec{A} = \myvec{-2\\1\\-1},
        \label{eq:chapters/12/10/2/17/dir-vec}
	\\
	    \implies 
	    \brak{\vec{B} - \vec{A}}^\top
	    \brak{\vec{C} - \vec{A}} = 0
    \end{align}
Hence, $\triangle ABC$ is right angled at $\vec{A}$.
